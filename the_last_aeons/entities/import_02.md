This means they are not only one of the largest legions, the better to enforce the will of the Tsar, but also the one whose influence is most widely felt among the stars. Their philosophy comes courtesy of their Imperator, the High Judge Siegfried van Banteinne. If Anubis is the sword of the Tsar, then Siegfried serves as his shield. His faith in the Tsars judgment is near unshakeable, and he brings this stalwart attitude to all of his endeavours. This means that he and the Paladins are very disciplined and extremely committed to their duties, but also means that they are inflexible in all things. Asking them to change their minds is like asking a stream to split a boulder: near impossible. The Paladins are split into three internal Orders: Richter, Schild, and Zweihander. Members of the Richter are expert infiltrators whose role is to keep their eyes and ears on the ground, aided by the inherent Paladin gene-blessing of shapeshifting. They serve as a spy network that spans the stars, although they are tight-lipped about exactly where and how many of their agents are deployed at any given time. If there are whispers of revolt among the populace, the Schild are called in to silence troublemakers and cut rebellion off at the root, acting as military police in order to destroy any and all dissent. It is only if the Richter and the Schild are faced with an overwhelming foe that the Zweihander are summoned into combat. The Zweihander are the Paladins elite fighting forces, and mistaking their lack of military accolades as a lack of military might is an error punishable by death. Firstly, thanks to the reach of the Richter it is not uncommon for the Zweihander to already know exactly where they must be and who they must kill in order to sow the most chaos in the enemy forces. Secondly, by the time they reach enemy forces they will already have been infiltrated by disguised members of the Schild, who can coldly and easily destroy the army from within. And finally, the Zweihander are unflinchingly loyal to the Tsar and to their Imperator. A member of the Paladins will fight until every last spark in their body is extinguished rather than yield victory to their foes. In terms of territory held, the Paladins are much less obsessed with squabbles over planetary control than the other Legions. Despite this, they hold strong influence over the worlds of the Beta Sector, so it is unofficially considered to be Paladin territory. When not on active duty the Paladins keep to themselves within the Gotterdammerung star system, heavily policed by the Zweihander if not otherwise occupied. Walkure is their official home planet, a near barren wasteland suitable only for the hardiest of recruits, and thus a fine training base for Paladin hopefuls. After the Heresy The Paladins worked to keep the Hegemony intact, attempting to keep out of the politics of Tsars and Regents. At the end of the five regencies, however, they took an active hand in forming the Federated Dominions of Humanity, becoming the Paladin Guard to watch over the Tsar-Elect.
